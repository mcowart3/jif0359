
To the Honourable Knight, Sir 

SIR,
THis is but a Postscript to the last Letter, and it is onely to tell you, that it was an impertinent jealousie that I conceived of that Gentlemans ab∣sence from my L. for he gives that full Te∣stimonie of him, that he never discerned any kinde of unfitnesse in him for any im∣ployment, except too much goodnesse; and Conscientiousnesse may sometimes make him somewhat lesse fit for some kindes of businesse, then a man of a looser raine. And this is all, that I conceive to have been in the commandment wherewith you ho∣noured

Your very humble and thankfull servant in Christ Jesus
John Donne:
2. Aug 1622.
